Candida albicans

Candida albicans is a yeast species commonly found in the human body. It may cause opportunistic candidiasis infections when the host's immune system becomes weakened or disrupted.

  • Category Yeast Fungus
  • Habitat Human Body / Mucosa
  • Common Infection Candidiasis
  • Risk Level Medium

Epidermophyton floccosum

This anthropophilic dermatophyte is commonly responsible for common skin conditions such as athlete's foot (tinea pedis), jock itch (tinea cruris), and ringworm transmissions.

  • Category Dermatophyte
  • Habitat Human Skin / Keratin
  • Common Infection Jock Itch & Tinea
  • Risk Level Medium

Trichophyton mentagrophytes

A highly infectious dermatophyte fungus primarily associated with acute inflammatory lesions, classic athlete's foot, ringworm infections, and cutaneous disorders.

  • Category Dermatophyte
  • Habitat Human & Animal Skin
  • Common Infection Ringworm / Tinea
  • Risk Level High Risk

Trichophyton rubrum

Recognized globally as one of the most dominant dermatophyte fungi. It is the leading cause of chronic fingernail infections (onychomycosis) and persistent athlete's foot.

  • Category Dermatophyte
  • Habitat Human Skin & Nails
  • Common Infection Chronic Athlete's Foot
  • Risk Level Medium
